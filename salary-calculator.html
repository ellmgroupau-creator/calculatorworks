Formula

monthly pay = annual salary ÷ 12
fortnightly pay = annual salary ÷ 26
weekly pay = annual salary ÷ 52
hourly pay = annual salary ÷ (52 × hours per week)

Example: An $85,000 annual salary over a 38-hour week is about $7,083.33 monthly, $3,269.23 fortnightly, $1,634.62 weekly, and $43.02 hourly.

How salary breakdowns help with budgeting

Knowing your pay at different intervals makes it easier to match income against expenses. Monthly figures help with rent, utilities, and subscriptions. Weekly or fortnightly amounts are useful if that is how often you are paid, and an hourly rate lets you compare job offers or evaluate overtime fairly.

Breaking an annual salary into smaller periods also highlights how much is available for saving or investing each pay cycle. Pairing this calculator with a savings or mortgage tool can help you see whether a given salary comfortably supports your financial goals.

Salary calculator FAQ

Does this calculator include tax?

No. The results show gross pay only. Tax rates vary by country, state, and individual circumstances, so the figures here represent your salary before any deductions. Use this as a starting point and consult a tax calculator or accountant for net (take-home) pay.

How is hourly pay calculated?

The calculator divides the annual salary by 52 weeks, then divides again by the number of hours you work per week. For example, an $80,000 salary at 40 hours per week gives $80,000 / (52 x 40) = $38.46 per hour.

Can I use this for contractor or freelance rates?

You can use it as a rough guide, but contractors typically need to account for self-employment tax, insurance, unpaid leave, and business expenses. A common rule of thumb is to add 25-30% on top of an equivalent employee salary when setting a freelance hourly rate.
