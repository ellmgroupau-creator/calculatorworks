Simple Interest Calculator

Formula

simple interest = principal × rate × time
rate = annual rate ÷ 100
final balance = principal + interest

Example: $10,000 at 5% simple interest for 5 years earns $2,500 in interest, for a final balance of $12,500.

Worked examples

$5,000 at 4% for 3 years = $600 interest, $5,600 total

$10,000 at 5% for 5 years = $2,500 interest, $12,500 total

$20,000 at 6% for 10 years = $12,000 interest, $32,000 total

FAQ

What is simple interest?

Simple interest applies the rate only to the original principal instead of growing on previously added interest.

When is simple interest more appropriate than compounding?

It is useful for straightforward examples, short-term scenarios, and products or exercises that do not compound.

Should I compare it with compound growth?

Yes. Comparing both methods shows how much extra growth compounding can create over time.
