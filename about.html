About CalculatorWorks

CalculatorWorks is a growing collection of free online calculators designed to make everyday calculations quicker, easier, and easier to trust.

What we do

CalculatorWorks is an independently operated website that provides free online calculators across four main categories: percentage calculators for working out increases, decreases, margins, and markups; finance tools for loans, mortgages, savings, and interest; unit conversion for length, weight, and temperature; and everyday calculators for tips, ages, dates, BMI, and GPA.

Our goal is to keep every tool simple to use, fast to load, and easy to understand. Each calculator page includes the relevant formula, a worked example, and a plain-language explanation so users can verify results for themselves.

Our approach

We focus on practical tools, clear formulas, and concise guidance. CalculatorWorks is intended as a helpful starting point for everyday planning, budgeting, conversions, and maths checks.

User-first design: Pages are built to load fast, work without signups, and keep the calculator or explanation as the main focus.

Monetisation approach: If advertising is added, placements will stay limited, clearly separated from content, and should not block results or key navigation.

Editorial standards

We hold every calculator page to a consistent set of standards before it is published:

  • Formulas shown alongside every calculator. Users can see exactly how the result is derived, not just the answer.
  • Worked examples for verification. Each page includes at least one step-by-step example so users can check the calculator against a known result.
  • Limitations stated clearly. We do not claim to provide tax calculations, professional financial advice, or medical guidance. Where a tool has constraints, they are noted on the page.
  • Content reviewed and corrected. When users report errors or suggest improvements through our contact page, we review and update the affected calculators.

How CalculatorWorks checks page quality

Each calculator page is built around a simple standard: the calculator should be easy to use, the formula should be visible, and the result should be explained in plain language. This helps visitors understand not only the final answer, but also the calculation behind it.

  • Input clarity: fields should make it obvious what value is required.
  • Result clarity: results should use sensible rounding and readable units.
  • Helpful context: pages should explain when the calculator is useful and when professional advice may be needed.
  • Maintenance: pages may be updated as formulas, examples, links, or user feedback identify better ways to explain a calculation.

Important note

The content on CalculatorWorks is general information only. It does not constitute financial, tax, legal, or medical advice. Calculator results are based on the formulas and inputs shown and may not account for every variable in a real-world situation.

You should always consult a qualified professional before making decisions that involve money, health, or legal matters. We accept no liability for actions taken based on the information or results provided on this site.
