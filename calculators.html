How to choose the right calculator

If you are solving shopping or classroom maths, start in Percentage Calculators. For repayments, savings goals, and pricing decisions, use Finance Calculators. If you need practical day-to-day tools, visit Everyday Calculators. This page is designed as a quick index so you can jump straight to the best-fit tool without searching page by page.

A good shortcut is to choose based on the result you want to walk away with. If you need a final sale price, use a discount tool. If you need a monthly borrowing figure, use a loan or mortgage tool. If you need to compare two numbers, use a percent-change tool. That sounds obvious, but it helps visitors avoid using a broad calculator when a more specific one will give a clearer answer.

All calculators FAQ

Can I browse calculators by topic?

Yes. CalculatorWorks groups tools into percentage, unit conversion, everyday, and finance categories so you can find related tools quickly.

Where should I start for loan, mortgage, or savings planning?

Go to Finance Calculators, then choose the Loan Repayment Calculator, Mortgage Calculator, or Savings Calculator.

Which calculators are best for margins and selling prices?

Use the Margin Calculator when you know selling price and cost, and the Markup Calculator when you know cost and desired markup percentage.
