Mortgage Calculator

Estimate monthly mortgage repayments plus total repayment and total interest using a standard amortisation formula.

Mortgage Calculator

Enter the mortgage amount, annual interest rate, and loan term to estimate monthly repayments.

Formula

monthly repayment = P × r × (1 + r)^n ÷ ((1 + r)^n - 1)
total repayment = monthly repayment × n
total interest = total repayment - principal

Example: A $500,000 mortgage at 6.2% over 30 years has an estimated monthly repayment of about $3,062.34.

Worked examples

$350,000 at 5.8% over 25 years = about $2,212.46 per month

$500,000 at 6.2% over 30 years = about $3,062.34 per month

$650,000 at 6% over 30 years = about $3,897.08 per month

How mortgage estimates help

Mortgage estimates are useful when setting a realistic buying budget, comparing fixed repayment scenarios, and understanding how interest affects the total amount repaid over time. Testing different loan sizes and rates can help you spot whether a property still fits your budget before you apply.

Mortgage calculator FAQ

What affects mortgage repayments the most?

The biggest factors are the loan amount, interest rate, and mortgage term. Even a small rate change can shift the monthly repayment and total interest noticeably.

Does this mortgage calculator include taxes or insurance?

No. This calculator focuses on principal and interest repayment estimates only, so use it as a planning guide rather than a full lender quote.

Should I compare a mortgage with savings goals?

Yes. Many buyers compare likely mortgage repayments with projected deposit growth using the Savings Calculator.

How to use mortgage results

Use the mortgage calculator to compare loan amount, interest rate, loan term, and repayment frequency. If the calculator includes extra repayments or deposits, test several scenarios so you can see the difference between a lower repayment and a lower total interest cost.

Before relying on the result: Actual mortgage repayments may include fees, offset account rules, redraw rules, insurance, taxes, rate changes, and lender-specific calculations.
