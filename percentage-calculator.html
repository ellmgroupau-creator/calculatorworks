Common percentage calculations

Percentages are used to compare a part with a whole. They are common in discounts, tax, tips, exam scores, interest rates, finance, business margins, and everyday comparisons. The key is identifying which number is the whole and which number is the part.

Quick percentage examples

  • 20% of 80 means 0.20 × 80 = 16.
  • 15 out of 60 means 15 ÷ 60 × 100 = 25%.
  • Increase from 50 to 65 means the change is 15, and 15 ÷ 50 × 100 = 30%.

Percentage calculator FAQ

How do I calculate X% of a number?

Multiply the number by the percentage and divide by 100. For example, 15% of 200 is 30.

When should I use this instead of percent change?

Use this calculator when you have one percentage and one base number. Use Percent Change Calculator when you are comparing an old value against a new value.

How to avoid percentage mistakes

Check whether the question asks for a percentage of a number, a percentage increase, a percentage decrease, markup, margin, or percentage points. These sound similar but use different calculations.

Before relying on the result: When comparing two percentages, be careful with wording. A move from 10% to 15% is a 5 percentage-point increase, but a 50% relative increase.
