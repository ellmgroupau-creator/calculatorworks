When to use this feet to metres converter

Feet and inches are still common in height charts, property listings, construction notes, and imported product specs, while metres are used in metric countries, engineering documents, and many official forms. This converter helps when you need a clean metric figure without doing the maths by hand.

  • Convert personal height from feet and inches into metres for forms, sports stats, and travel documents.
  • Check room, furniture, or ladder measurements when one source uses imperial units and another uses metric units.
  • Use the quick reference table below for common height lookups before using the full calculator for precise values.

Feet to metres FAQ

How many metres are in one foot?

One foot equals exactly 0.3048 metres. When inches are included, convert the total height to feet first or let the calculator do it for you.

Why does this converter ask for inches as well?

Many height measurements are written as feet and inches, such as 5'10" or 6'2". Including inches gives you a more accurate metric result.

Is this good for room and building measurements?

Yes. It works for any feet-based measurement, including room dimensions, ladder heights, and other building specs.
