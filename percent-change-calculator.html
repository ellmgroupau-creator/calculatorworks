Formula

percent change = ((new value - original value) ÷ original value) × 100

Example: from 80 to 100 = 25.000%

Worked examples

From 80 to 100 = 25.000%

From 50 to 45 = -10.000%

From 200 to 260 = 30.000%

When percent change is the right tool

Use percent change when you are comparing two values and want to describe the movement between them. That makes it useful for price shifts, sales performance, traffic changes, weight changes, grades, and other before-and-after comparisons.

If your question is really “what is X% of Y?”, use the Percentage Calculator instead. If your question is “what is the final price after a discount?”, the Discount Calculator will usually be more useful than a raw percent-change result.

Percent change FAQ

When is percent change negative?

It is negative when the new value is lower than the original value. That shows a decrease rather than an increase.

Why does the original value matter so much?

Because percent change measures the difference relative to where you started. The same raw change can represent a small or large percentage depending on the original value.

What if I only need to calculate a percentage of one number?

Use the Percentage Calculator for that. Percent change is specifically for comparing one value against another.
