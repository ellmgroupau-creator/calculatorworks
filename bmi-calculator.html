Formula

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height (m)²

Example: 70 kg ÷ 1.75² = 22.86

BMI weight categories

Under 18.5 — Underweight

18.5 – 24.9 — Normal weight

25.0 – 29.9 — Overweight

30.0 and above — Obese

Worked examples

60 kg, 165 cm → BMI = 22.04 (Normal weight)

85 kg, 180 cm → BMI = 26.23 (Overweight)

50 kg, 160 cm → BMI = 19.53 (Normal weight)

FAQ

What does BMI measure?

BMI estimates body mass relative to height using a standard formula and is commonly used as a quick screening reference.

Is BMI medical advice?

No. It is a general indicator only and should not replace personalised medical advice or diagnosis.

When is this calculator useful?

It is useful for quick comparisons, fitness tracking, and understanding how the BMI formula responds to different inputs.
