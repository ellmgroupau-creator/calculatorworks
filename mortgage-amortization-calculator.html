Formula

Monthly repayment = P × r × (1 + r)^n ÷ ((1 + r)^n - 1). First-month interest = P × r.

For a $500,000 loan at 6.2% over 30 years, the calculator estimates the monthly repayment and shows how much of the early payment is interest versus principal.

How to use this calculator

Use this calculator when you want more than a basic repayment number. It helps show how a mortgage payment is split between principal and interest at the start of the loan, which is useful when comparing loan terms or understanding why early mortgage payments are often interest-heavy.

For important money decisions, treat this as a planning estimate. Real outcomes can change because of fees, taxes, repayment timing, lender rules, product features, and market conditions.
