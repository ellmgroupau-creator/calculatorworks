Finance calculators FAQ

Which finance calculator should I use for a home loan?

Use the Mortgage Calculator for monthly repayment, total repayment, and total interest estimates on a home loan.

What is the difference between margin and markup?

Margin is profit as a percentage of selling price, while markup is the added amount as a percentage of cost. CalculatorWorks has separate tools for both.

Can I compare borrowing and saving scenarios on the site?

Yes. Use the Loan Repayment Calculator or Mortgage Calculator for borrowing, then compare them with the Savings Calculator or Savings Goal Calculator.

Finance calculator guide

Use the finance calculators to compare repayments, estimate savings targets, understand interest, and test budget decisions before committing to them. The most useful finance pages are usually the ones that let you adjust assumptions such as interest rate, term, payment amount, and starting balance.

Important: Finance results are estimates only. Real loan, tax, superannuation, bank, or investment outcomes can vary because of fees, compounding rules, lender policies, repayment timing, and local regulations.

How to choose the right finance calculator

Finance calculators are most useful when they answer one clear question at a time. If you are borrowing money, start with repayment and affordability tools. If you are saving or investing, start with savings, compound interest, and return calculators. If you are managing debt, focus on payoff time, interest cost, and payment comparisons.

Recommended starting points

  • Saving: Savings Calculator, Savings Goal Calculator, and Emergency Fund Calculator.
